class="rendered_qtext"><p class="qtext_para">Bud Heytt gave you the questions you should ask a TPM/Project Engineer/whatever you call it in your organization.&nbsp; Or are you looking for a Senior Systems Engineer?</p><p class="qtext_para">Unfortunately, you have a bigger question to ask, and it&#039;s right there in your question.</p><p class="qtext_para">WHAT DO YOU WANT YOUR TPM TO DO?!??!?!</p><p class="qtext_para">Until you can answer that question for yourself, then you&#039;re just hiring people at random, because your Job Description for the position is going to be vague, and the person you want can snow you.&nbsp; One way or the other, you&#039;re probably going to have to grow someone into the role as you define it.&nbsp; I would actually start with your engineering team, as they probably understand your business better than anyone else you bring in could (I&#039;m assuming you&#039;re a small organization that&#039;s growing.)</p><p class="qtext_para">Questions to ask yourself:</p><p class="qtext_para">1) Is my TPM part of my Leadership Team?&nbsp; Do they have people management responsibilities, set the strategic direction for our future business development, or am I looking for someone who&#039;s just extremely technical who isn&#039;t going to piss off the technical people?</p><p class="qtext_para">2) How does my TPM differ from a Project Manager?&nbsp; Am I really looking for someone who can manage a customer&#039;s technical issues and lead a team, or am I looking for some who can break the ties when my Senior technical folks are about to go Medieval on each other&#039;s asses?</p><p class="qtext_para">3) Can the person in this role identify Contract issues and work with our Legal folks, and a PM, or at least identify those issues for the person responsible for those issues, and do they care?</p><p class="qtext_para">4) Schedule.&nbsp; Budget.&nbsp; Schedule.&nbsp; Budget.&nbsp; And just for a change of pace, Schedule and Budget.&nbsp; Who handles those?&nbsp; The TPM?</p><p class="qtext_para">5) If you&#039;re actually manufacturing something (including software,) does this person have to understand the manufacturing process, and can bridge the gap between production, configuration management, quality control and quality assurance?&nbsp; Is an understanding of Safety and Security a pre-requisite?&nbsp; Oh, and don&#039;t get me started on the difference between Requirements Management and Requirements Development...</p><p class="qtext_para">I could go on and on, but these are the things that you have to understand for YOUR business before you go looking for a TPM.&nbsp; It&#039;s more than just about getting a technical person who can manage schedules and budgets.&nbsp; Even with an established business, you have to have a strategy to grow people into that role, or evolve the role to off-load you from those things.<br /></p></span><div id='TiaOQm'><div
